Deep learning models are increasingly deployed in high-stakes areas like healthcare, autonomous driving, and finance. However, their internal representations are notoriously opaque, leading to widespread concern over accountability and safety. This paper introduces an explainable AI framework designed to audit convolutional layers for systemic bias. Our primary contribution is a localized activation mapping technique that visualizes feature weights relative to protected variables. Across three demographic datasets, our method successfully exposed latent bias in 84% of pre-trained image classifiers, paving the way for more equitable deep learning deployments.
